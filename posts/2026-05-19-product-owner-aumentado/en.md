Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the fastest-growing technology in human history, often compared to the invention of electricity. For a Product Owner (PO), AI is not just an automation tool - it's a strategic partner that amplifies decision-making and innovation.

## Redefining the PO's postures

Bringing AI into the workflow reshapes traditional responsibilities and frees the PO to focus on what truly matters: strategy and value.

- **The Visionary** - uses AI and the 3x3 framework to craft narratives that align stakeholders around a clear direction.
- **The Experimenter** - AI makes it possible to build low-fidelity prototypes and mock-ups in minutes, speeding up hypothesis validation before a single line of code is written.
- **The Orchestrator** (a new posture) - delegates routine tasks to AI (email drafts, meeting summaries, user-story writing), freeing time for high-impact work.
- **The Customer Representative** - AI helps draft personas and map values, but those are hypotheses, not truths. They must always be validated with real people.

## Context engineering and precision

The quality of the output depends on the quality of the instruction. We've moved from simple *prompt engineering* to **Context Engineering** - designing workflows that let AI reason and adapt over time.

To keep facts reliable and avoid "hallucinations", we rely on **RAG** (*Retrieval-Augmented Generation*): a technique that lets AI consult external and private company knowledge bases to produce contextual answers.

## Ethics, security and sustainability

Leading with AI carries added responsibility. The **subscription principle** is vital: in free tools, the user's data is often the product; in professional tiers, the tool is the product and privacy is protected.

The PO must also stay aware of the massive environmental impact of training and running these models, and promote responsible use.

The future of Product Ownership isn't being replaced by AI - it's evolving into a leadership profile that uses the technology to solve real problems more efficiently and more ethically.
